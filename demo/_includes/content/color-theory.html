Introduction

This project started from a need to address visual disabilities and improve web accessibility within a design system I've been working on since 2013. The heart of this effort is @rgbyk/color, a key piece of the application that helps make colors work better for everyone.

It integrates various color systems, primarily adopting Munsell's Color System.

Familiarity with Munsell's theory will reveal enhancements such as the expansion to 12 hues from the traditional 10 and a modernized color naming convention.

The framework conducts in-depth analysis along a cylindrical model to assess the hue, lightness, and saturation of colors. This process involves measuring human visual responses to ensure uniformity in color perception, allowing for adjustments to achieve optimal results.

Determining the spacing between colors along these dimensions happens by taking measurements of human visual responses. In each of these dimensions, the colors are as close to uniform as we could make them perceptually, but there are plenty of variables that can be adjusted for perfection.

Value Represents the measurement vertically, akin to ascending a pole.
Chroma Denotes the measurement horizontally, diverging from a vertical reference point.
Hue Signifies the measurement encircling a circle, representing the color's inherent character.

01. Hues

Hue = the measurement around a circle.

The 12 major colors of the color wheel, at 30° intervals on the HSV color wheel. This constitutes the complete set of primary, secondary, and tertiary color names.

  • Red (0° or 360°)
  • Orange (30°)
  • Yellow (60°)
  • Chartreuse Green (90°)
  • Green (120°)
  • Spring Green (150°)
  • Cyan (180°)
  • Azure (210°)
  • Blue (240°)
  • Violet (270°)
  • Magenta (300°)
  • Rose (330°)

02. Values

Value = the measurement up a vertical pole.

Value, or "lightness", varies vertically along the color solid, from black ($value-0) at the bottom, to white ($value-10) at the top.

Neutral grays lie along the vertical axis between black and white.

03. Chromas

Chroma = the measurement on horizontal, away from a vertical pole.

Chromas, measured radially from the center of each slice, represents the "purity" of a color (relating to Saturation). with lower chroma being less pure (more washed out, as in pastels).
